## Goal
Implement the rule that couples a credit-card purchase to its payment category:
when money is spent on a card against a normal spending category, budgeted money
moves into that card's payment category.

## Public API to produce
```dart
// lib/domain/budgeting/credit_card_movement.dart
/// The signed amount to add to a credit-card account's **payment category**
/// `available` for one transaction recorded on that card and categorised to a
/// normal spending category.
///
/// [transactionAmount] is signed (purchase negative, refund positive).
/// [categoryAvailableBeforeTransaction] is the spending category's `available`
/// immediately before this transaction is applied.
Money creditCardPaymentMovement({
  required Money transactionAmount,
  required Money categoryAvailableBeforeTransaction,
});

/// The portion of a credit-card purchase that the spending category could NOT
/// cover — i.e. credit overspending. Always >= 0. Consumed by M2-T12.
Money creditOverspendingPortion({
  required Money transactionAmount,
  required Money categoryAvailableBeforeTransaction,
});
```

## Implementation notes
- **Purchase (`transactionAmount < 0`).** Let `spend = transactionAmount.abs()`
  and `funded = max(Money.zero(), categoryAvailableBeforeTransaction)`.
  - `creditCardPaymentMovement = +min(spend, funded)` — the funded portion of
    the spend moves into the payment category (`docs/04`).
  - `creditOverspendingPortion = spend − min(spend, funded)` — the remainder the
    category could not cover.
- **Charge to the payment category itself (interest)** and **paying the card (a
  transfer)** are *not* inputs to this function — they are ordinary activity on
  the payment category, handled by `computeCategoryActivity` (M2-T03) and the
  balance calculator. Callers must only invoke this function for a transaction
  categorised to a *normal* spending category on a credit-card account.
- **Refund (`transactionAmount > 0`).** A refund on the card returns money to
  the spending category and should pull the mirror amount back out of the
  payment category: `creditCardPaymentMovement = −min(transactionAmount,
  <prior payment-category contribution>)`. The exact cap depends on how much was
  previously moved in, which this stateless function does not know.
  **NEEDS REVIEW:** `docs/04` does not specify the refund cap precisely. For
  this task, implement the refund as `−transactionAmount` (full reversal) and
  flag it; the orchestrator confirms the rule before M2-T16. For a refund,
  `creditOverspendingPortion` is `Money.zero()`.
- `Money` arithmetic only — no `double`.

## Tests to write
File: `test/domain/budgeting/credit_card_movement_test.dart`
Use hand-computed milliunit values.
- **Purchase, fully funded** — category had ≥ the spend: movement equals the
  full spend; overspending portion is zero.
- **Purchase, partially funded** — category had less than the spend: movement
  equals the funded portion; overspending portion equals the remainder.
- **Purchase, unfunded** — category `available` was zero or negative: movement
  is zero; overspending portion equals the whole spend.
- **Refund** — a positive `transactionAmount`: movement is negative per the
  NEEDS REVIEW rule above; overspending portion is zero.
- A zero `transactionAmount` yields zero movement and zero overspending.
